HP printer series

Which HP printer series do you have?

HP produces several product lines. Each has different connectivity features, ink systems, and common issues.

🖨️

HP DeskJet

Entry-level inkjet printers for home use. DeskJet printers are compact and affordable. Common issues include wireless connectivity drops and cartridge recognition errors after installing refilled ink.

🖨️

HP OfficeJet Pro

Mid-range inkjet AIO printers for home offices and small businesses. OfficeJet Pro models support automatic duplex, ADF scanning, and network printing. Common issues include offline status after router restarts and driver conflicts after Windows updates.

⚡

HP LaserJet

Monochrome and color laser printers for offices. LaserJet printers are fast and produce sharp text. Common issues include paper jams on duplex printing and toner cartridge errors when using third-party toner.

📸

HP ENVY

Consumer inkjet printers with photo quality output. ENVY models are designed for home users who print photos. Common issues include high ink consumption and offline errors when HP Instant Ink subscription service has a billing issue.

🛢️

HP Smart Tank

HP ink tank printers with large refillable reservoirs for low per-page cost. Smart Tank printers are well suited for frequent home printing. Common issues include clogged printheads after extended periods without printing.

🏢

HP PageWide

High-speed inkjet printers for business use. PageWide printers produce laser-like speeds with inkjet quality. Common issues include network configuration in enterprise environments and firmware update failures.

Common HP issues

Step-by-step fixes for the most reported HP printer problems.

1

HP printer shows offline on Windows

Open Settings, go to Bluetooth and devices, Printers and scanners. Select your HP printer. Click Open print queue. Cancel any stuck jobs. Go back and click Printer properties, then check that Use Printer Offline is not checked under the Printer menu. If the option says Use Printer Online, click it to bring the printer back online. HP also provides the HP Print and Scan Doctor tool — a free diagnostic utility that detects and automatically fixes most HP offline errors on Windows.

2

HP printer not connecting to Wi-Fi

On the printer panel, go to Settings, Wireless Setup Wizard. Select your network from the list and enter the password. If the network does not appear, confirm the printer is within range of the 2.4 GHz band of your router. HP printers do not support 5 GHz Wi-Fi. If WPS is available on your router, use it instead: press WPS on the router, then select WPS on the printer within 2 minutes for automatic pairing without entering a password.

3

HP driver installation fails

Download the latest HP full feature software and driver from support.hp.com. Search for your exact model number. Select your operating system. Download and run the installer as administrator. If the installer stops partway through, temporarily disable your antivirus, then run the installer again. After installation, restart the computer before adding the printer.

4

HP ink cartridge error or not recognized

Remove the ink cartridge from the printer and inspect the gold contacts on the cartridge for smudges or damage. Clean the contacts gently with a dry lint-free cloth. Reinstall the cartridge firmly until it clicks. If the error continues, the cartridge may be empty, defective, or incompatible. HP printers are designed to work with genuine HP cartridges — some models will show errors or print restrictions when non-HP ink is installed.

HP-specific tools

Official HP diagnostic utilities that help fix common problems.

HP Print and Scan Doctor

A free Windows application from HP that diagnoses and automatically fixes printer connection, offline, queue, and driver issues. Download it from support.hp.com. It is the fastest first step for any HP printer problem on Windows.

Works with most HP DeskJet, OfficeJet, ENVY, and LaserJet models on Windows 10 and Windows 11.

HP Smart app

The HP Smart app is available for Windows, macOS, iOS, and Android. It provides setup guidance, printer status, ink level monitoring, scan to phone, and access to HP Instant Ink from a single interface. It is the recommended setup tool for new HP printers.

Download from the Microsoft Store, Apple App Store, or Google Play. Requires an HP account for some features.

HP Embedded Web Server

HP network printers have a built-in web interface accessible from any browser on the same network. Enter the printer IP address in your browser to access print settings, ink levels, network configuration, and firmware update options without installing any software.

Find the IP address by printing a network configuration page from the printer settings menu.

HP firmware update

Outdated printer firmware causes compatibility issues with new operating system versions and security vulnerabilities. Update the printer firmware from the HP Smart app, from the printer embedded web server, or by downloading the firmware file from support.hp.com and installing it manually.

Do not power off the printer during a firmware update — this can permanently damage the device.
